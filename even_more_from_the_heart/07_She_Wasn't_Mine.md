“She Wasn’t Mine” reflects on the end of a marriage that began with the spark that inspired the song “First Glance.”

Some stories begin with a moment that feels almost effortless. One conversation. One connection. One of those rare first meetings where everything just seems to click.

That was the beginning of our story.

When we met, there was an instant connection. The kind of moment that later inspired the song “First Glance.” Conversation was easy. The chemistry was natural. It felt like two people who were meant to meet had simply found each other at the right time.

That connection grew into a relationship and eventually into marriage. For a while it felt like we were building something real together.

Then life took us in a different direction.

She chose to join the Air Force Reserve and left for training. What was supposed to be a temporary stretch of distance slowly became something else. During that time away she met someone new, and the path we had been walking together quietly began to change.

From my perspective it felt sudden. One moment you believe the future you are building together is solid. The next moment you are watching that future move in a direction you never expected.

The first instinct is anger. It is easy to believe someone stepped in and took something that belonged to you. Anger wants a simple explanation and someone to blame.

But time has a way of revealing a harder truth.

No one can take a heart that is fully committed.

If someone leaves, it is not because they were stolen away. It is because they made a choice. That realization does not erase the pain and it does not make divorce easy to carry. But it does bring clarity.

Love only exists as long as two people keep choosing it.

This song reflects the moment that understanding finally settles in. The point where you stop trying to explain the ending and start accepting that sometimes people simply choose a different road.

There is pain in that truth, but there is also freedom.

You cannot hold on to someone who no longer wants to stay. The only thing left to protect is your own peace and the life you continue building after the dust settles.

Looking back now, I understand something I could not see then.

The connection we shared was real. The beginning mattered.

But the ending revealed the truth I had to learn.

She wasn’t mine.

She was simply someone whose path crossed mine for a while before continuing in another direction.
